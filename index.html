Why Computer Science?

Computer programming is creative. It allows us to change the world! Students of all skill ranges are welcome to take computer science classes at SHA. Computer science is a top paying career and computer programming jobs are growing at two times the national average. Many top colleges and companies are making efforts to recruit female computer science students, making it an ideal time for women to pursue computer science degrees.

Computer science is a field that allows students to explore problem-solving and design in highly personal ways. The hands-on learning is engaging, applicable to the real world, and outstanding preparation for virtually all careers in STEM. Coding projects provide students with opportunities to unify their logical and creative thought processes and to work in teams that leverage each member's unique strengths.

Computer Science Concentration at SHA

The Computer Science Concentration is a concentration that begins freshmen year. Courses in computer science are taken each year. No prior experience is necessary. Students should sign up for the computer science concentration if they are genuinely interested in learning about:

  • Technology
  • Design
  • Building apps
  • Coding
  • Making games
  • Making websites

Computer Science Class Offerings

  • Year 1: Computer Science Essentials

    Students are introduced to computer science, coding fundamentals, and essential computational thinking practices. The course begins with block coding for app development and gradually transitions to text-based programming in Python.
  • Year 2: AP Computer Science Principles

    Using Python as a primary tool and incorperating multiple platforms and languages for computation, this course aims to further develop computational thinking skills, generate excitement about career paths in computing, and introduce professional tools that foster creativity and collaboration. Projects include app development, visualization of data, cybersecurity, and simulation.
  • Year 3: AP Computer Science A

    Students will cultivate their understanding of coding through analyzing, writing, and testing code as they explore concepts like modularity, variables, and control structures. The course emphasizes object-oriented programming and design using the Java programming language.
  • Year 4: Cybersecurity

    This course exposes students to the growing and far-reaching field of cybersecurity. Student learning focuses on personal security, system security, network security, and applied cybersecurity, such as digital forensics.
